How Credit Works.

Credit scores are important and used for everyhting from overdrafts and credit cards to mobile-phone deals crucially, mortgages. 6 Months before you apply for a mortgage, ensure that you have created a wholesome credit portrait of yourself to make this process easier!

You can improve your credit score by doing the following:

  • Start by checking your credit score through one or all of the three main credit-reference agencies mentioned above: Equifax, Experian and Callcredit
  • Make sure you are on the electoral roll. Make sure your name is spelt right and all your address history is correct and up to date
  • Get a credit score and use it in a relaxed manner. If you have a poor credit score becuase you have not had credit in the past, take out a credit card and use it on groceries for example. Set up direct debits to cleat it off in full every month
  • Do not get a payday loan, this is called the credit-score killer. Some banks will not lend to you at all if you have taken out a payday loan

The Property Ladder.

Do you want to buy your own property? Do you know how a mortgage works? Unsure of the financial and legal obligations?

We are here to provide the answers to all of your questions.

To begin with, a deposit for your desired property will cost approximately 5% to 20% of its total value. You will also have to consider hidden costs, including (but not limited to):

  • Survey costs
  • Solicitor fees
  • Removal costs
  • Buildings insurance
  • Initial furnishing and decorating costs
  • Mortgage arrangement and valuation fees

It is also important that you can afford all of your monthly repayments as a first-time home buyer. If you cannot commit to these payments, then you have to consider whether you can actually afford to take your first step onto the property ladder.
