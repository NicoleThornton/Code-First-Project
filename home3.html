Boost your Credit Score.


A credit score is a numerical expression based on a level analysis of a person's credit files, to represent the creditworthiness of an individual. Your credit score is a number that represents the risk a lender takes when you borrow money.

For this reason, credit scores are really important and determine whether banks will issue you an overdraft, credit card or even a loan for a mortgage. Credit scores are also used by telecomunications companies to determine if they should issue you a mobile phone contract.

You can improve your credit score by doing the following:

  • Check your credit score via credit-reference agencies including Equifax, Experian or Callcredit
  • Make sure your name is on the electoral roll.
  • Do not get a payday loan, also known as a credit-score killer. Some banks will not lend to you at all if you have taken out a payday loan.

The Property Ladder.

Do you want to buy your own property? Know how a mortgage works? Unsure of the financial and legal obligations?

We are here to provide the answers to all of your questions.

To begin with, a deposit for your desired property will cost approximately 5% to 20% of its total value. You will also have to consider hidden costs, including (but not limited to):

  • Survey costs
  • Solicitor fees
  • Removal costs
  • Buildings insurance
  • Initial furnishing and decorating costs
  • Mortgage arrangement and valuation fees

It is also important that you can afford all of your monthly repayments as a first-time home buyer. If you cannot commit to these payments, then you have to consider whether you can actually afford to take your first step onto the property ladder. Use our financial calculator to calculate how much you have to save and for how long to make sure you take this step at the right time.
