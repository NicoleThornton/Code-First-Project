A guide to saving vs spending

Finding a budgeting tool is essential to knowing much you are spending as it allows you maintain control over your spending and allows you to be flexible when you need to.You may be questioning yourself whether you really need a budget and the ultimate reason is that it can help you make the maximise your money, get out of a financial situation, plan ahead, live the life you want and achieve peace of mind! As well as having a good credit rating, it will allow you to ponder over your spending habits and re-focus your financial goals.Take back the control.When your budget your money wisely, you'll be less likely to be caught out of unexpected costs and end up in debt!

Now is a great time to review your budgeting goals you had set for yourself last year and figure out what worked for you and what didn’t. If you haven’t yet begun to budget, this is a great time to start!

  1. Avoid temptation and follow a list of the necessary weekly items or essential shopping purchases
  2. Carry extra cash with you and leave your debit/credit cards at home
  3. Set your priorities, and number them by focusing on your needs, wants and other money for debts/savings
  4. Pay your bills first
  5. Work out your regular income and give yourself a weekly allowance
  6. Keep your receipts and bills organised and review them weekly
  7. Align your budget with your goals and values
  8. Lastly, make a spreadsheet for your regular purchases and list the items that you buy the most.Make a target to spend an allocated amount of money for each category;debt,education,entertainment,food,events, housing, insurance, utilities, transport, savings, healthcare, appointments and emergencies. Categorise a colour code assorted to each different paycheck and then assign a budget in your credit card or amount of cash to spend on within each month.After categorising your spending,you can then put the allocated cash in different spending envelopes or remind yourself that you have a limited allowance for each category. A useful tip is to overestimate each allocated budget by 10 percent to give yourself a safety margin.

Severe debt?


If you’ve passed the date to pay off your missed credit card or loan payments or if you’re behind with so-called ‘priority debts’ such as your: rent, mortgage, fines, bills, tax/credits, do not borrow any money First contact your local free non-profit debt crisis agencies named as Citizens Advice Bureau, StepChange,Debt Charity or National Debtline in order to figure out what steps you should take to make repayments or how to recieve support for your remaining installments

  • If you didn’t buy, for example, a car or made any large purchases, but you've used cards or loans to fill gaps, you should be extra cautious.
  • Do a budget to see where you are wasting cash; you may be leaking money on unused subs or on costly subscriptions.
  • Work out how much savings you have to cover the debt excluding your necessities and then work out how much longer it will take you to pay off your debt or increase your savings if you give in
  • Keep a debt diary.Write a note of when you’re likely to over spend.If you can identify trends that lead to overspending you can prevent them easily by replacing them with a solution.Completely avoid even stepping into shopping malls or why not do an activity that is worthwhile like your hobbies?
  • Get in touch with friends and family to see if they can offer any support and advice and contact your local free non-profit debt agencies to improve your credit rating.
