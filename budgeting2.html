Spending VS Saving

Stressed lately? Bills pilling up or car problems? Or that dream vacation your best friends are planning? You may be questioning yourself whether you really need a budget and the ultimate answer is YES.A budgeting plan is an invaluable tool designed to support you within your means to manage your money.Many people associate it with restrictions and hassle where in fact budgeting can be simple.
Planning and monitoring your budget will identify wasteful expenses,allowing you to save more and adapt as quickly as your financial situation changes.Having this sense of financial clarity is something you NEED throughout life.When you realise the breakdown of your expenses, you may be surprised how things really add up.As well as scrutinising everything big and small, you can also move your focus on keeping things that are important to you, still reward yourself and most importantly, keeping yourself out of debt!

It's time to whip out a pen at hand and a note book.Follow these simple steps to create a solid action plan that actually works!

  • Step 1:Align your budget with your goals and values
  • By defining your goals, it is the first step to help you be motivated whether it is to save money for your future or stop overspending.Make sure your goals are also SMART.

  • Step 2:Distinguish your income and your expenses
  • The first step is to work out your regular income and based on this, you will give yourself a weekly allowance, and try to be realistic
  • List your sources of income(including the money that goes into your bank account)such as money you earned from work, from family/friends and work out your expenses such as(including money that goes out your bank account)such as student loans,bills, utilties
    • This works if your a cash user or a credit card user and you can easily check where you have been spending your money.
  • Make a spreadsheet for your regular purchases and list the items that you buy the most/transactions.After you have categorized your expenses,total each category by month.This will give you an idea of your current spending.
  • Make a target to spend an allocated amount of money for each category;debt,education,entertainment,food,events, housing, insurance, utilities, transport, savings, healthcare, appointments and emergencies. A useful tip is to overestimate each allocated budget by 10 percent to give yourself a safety margin.
  • Compile a colour code assorted to each different pay check and then assign a budget in your credit card or amount of cash to spend on within each month. After categorising your spending, you can then put the allocated cash in different spending envelopes or remind yourself that you have a limited allowance for each category.
  • You can also download an app to help you track your spending monthly and quarterly. You can recieve a printout of the Budget Planner or download it on Excel which is great for monitoring your money for the long-term.

  • Step 3:Any savings?
  • Set your priorities, and number them by focusing on your needs, wants and other money for debts/savings
  • Carry extra cash with you and leave your debit/credit cards at home
  • Prioritizing bills will allow you to save more money.Set aside a contingency fund in a bank account which delivers the best rate of interest.The amount will vary depending on the idfference between your spending and income.
  • Find out your weakness.From what is left of discretionary spending.select 1-3 areas of spending depending on how much your are able to balance; to spend on your hobbies, luxuries or other things that let you unplug and relax such as camping trips.Figure out what else to cut back!


  • Step 4: Progress, adjust and evaluate?
  • Keep your reciepts organised and review them weekly
  • If you see large numbers indicating higher expenses, you may be shocked so create a reasonable budget for each category such as food,clothing, etc.
  • To prevent yourself from overspending, and to reduce your expenses, limit necessities or find alternatives

Pain-free saving?

  • Being a better consumer; browse at sales online to see where can get this weeks items at the best price
  • Stick loose change in jar and at the end of the month pay it into your bank account.If you have the time to go to various stores or browse online, then you can adjust your spending money
  • Plan your meals and shop ahead to save money travelling and dine on offers.
  • To trim your budget, find alternatives on your car insurance, subscriptions,memberships, like community yoga classes are much more worth it than private oone-to-one yoga sessions.
  • If your tempted to shop or have buy `that bag you had laid your eyes on`, work out how long it would take you to earn that money in hours worked as well as taking on thhe account tax.
  • To save more than you spent in the long-term, storing money aside for emergencies is a large-time cruncher, or possible purchases in the future such as buying a car, having children, driving, studying for a degree or for a holiday away!

Painful-saving?

  • Cut down on one-time purchases and reduce your annual monthly expenses on leisure, food and perhaps a part time job/eating in will reduce your overspending.Those mixed drinks after Friday can really add onto your expenses.
  • For every new thing you buy, donate or sell one of your items
  • Avoid debts and get your tax right
  • Reduce expensive vices and when you identify what it is that you spend on necessities, over-estimate the price for them.
  • To save money on food, use coupons and buy items in bulk
  • Gas/electricity:As online deals are raised higher,you can save a massive amount by comparing deals and switching to the cheapestdeals, but hurry!. Severe debt?

If you’ve passed the date to pay off your missed credit card or loan payments or if you’re behind with so-called ‘priority debts’ such as your: rent, mortgage, fines, bills, tax/credits.First contact your local free non-profit debt crisis agencies named as HM Revenue & Customs,Citizens Advice Bureau, StepChange,Debt Charity,Debt Advice Foundation or National Debtline in order to figure out what steps you should take to make repayments or how to recieve support for your remaining installments.

  1. If you didn’t buy, for example, a car or made any large purchases, but you've used cards or loans to fill gaps, you should be extra cautious.
  2. Do a budget to see where you are wasting cash; you may be leaking money on unused subs or on costly subscriptions.
  3. Work out how much savings you have to cover the debt excluding your necessities and then work out how much longer it will take you to pay off your debt or increase your savings if you give in
  4. Keep a debt diary.Write a note of when you’re likely to over spend.If you can identify trends that lead to overspending you can prevent them easily by replacing them with a solution.Completely avoid even stepping into shopping malls or why not do an activity that is worthwhile like your hobbies?
  5. Get in touch with friends and family to see if they can offer any tips.Do not borrow any moeny from friends and family if you cannot afford to pay them back.
  6. Your may have a bigger debt problem if you are worried you can't pay back money you owe if it is affecting your living conditions.Contact your local free non-profit agencies now to improve your credit rating and recieve up-to-date advice about how debt affects you and support to practical solutions.
